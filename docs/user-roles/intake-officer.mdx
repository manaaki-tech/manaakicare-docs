# Intake Officer Role

Understand the intake officer role, including capabilities, responsibilities, and dashboard features.

## Overview

Intake Officers are responsible for managing the <Term path="referral" plural /> intake process - from receiving new <Term path="referral" plural /> to dispatching them for service delivery. They serve as the gateway for <Term path="serviceUser" plural /> entering the Manaaki Central system.

## Key Responsibilities

### <Term path="referral" plural /> Processing
- Receiving and reviewing new <Term path="referral" plural />
- Verifying <Term path="serviceUser" plural /> information accuracy
- Assessing <Term path="referral" plural /> priority and urgency
- Ensuring all required documentation is collected

### Assessment & Review
- Conducting initial intake assessments
- Reviewing consent documentation
- Evaluating service eligibility
- Identifying appropriate services for <Term path="serviceUser" plural />

### Service Dispatch
- Matching <Term path="serviceUser" plural /> with appropriate services
- Coordinating with service teams
- Managing <Term path="referral" plural /> workflow progression
- Ensuring timely service allocation

### Documentation
- Maintaining accurate <Term path="referral" plural /> records
- Uploading and organizing supporting documents
- Recording notes and observations
- Tracking <Term path="referral" plural /> history

## Workflow Overview

```
New Entry → Draft → Submitted → In Review → Service Review → Dispatched → Active/Completed
```

### Status Progression

1. **Draft** - Entry created but not yet submitted
2. **Submitted** - Entry submitted for processing
3. **Pending Review** - Awaiting supervisor review
4. **Pending Service Review** - Approved, awaiting service allocation
5. **Awaiting Service** - Dispatched, waiting for service to begin
6. **Completed** - Service delivery completed

## Best Practices

### Prioritization
- Process **Urgent** and **Priority** <Term path="referral" plural /> first
- Review the priority queue at the start of each day
- Escalate urgent cases that require immediate attention

### Data Quality
- Verify <Term path="serviceUser" plural /> contact information
- Ensure all required fields are completed
- Check for duplicate <Term path="serviceUser" plural /> records before creating new ones

### Communication
- Document all <Term path="serviceUser" plural /> communications
- Keep referrers informed of progress
- Coordinate with supervisors on complex cases

### Timeliness
- Aim to process new <Term path="referral" plural /> within 24-48 hours
- Follow up on stalled <Term path="referral" plural />
- Monitor the In Review queue for delays
